Analysis of CVSS: Characteristics and Alternatives

Spring et al. (2021) highlight several weaknesses of the CVSS. Firstly, the calculation formula lacks empirical justification, raising questions about the validity of the resulting scores. Additionally, the system struggles to account for the context of vulnerabilities, leading to inaccurate assessments. Finally, CVSS often confuses severity with risk, a common mistake that impacts the prioritization of security actions (Spring et al., 2021).

Critical Issues with CVSS

  • Contextual Incompatibility: CVSS does not adapt well to specific environments like embedded or safety-critical systems. For example, vulnerabilities that affect system availability can have catastrophic consequences in healthcare (Chase and Coley, 2019).
  • Chained Vulnerabilities: CVSS evaluates vulnerabilities in isolation, failing to capture risks posed by complex attacks that exploit multiple vulnerabilities in succession (Farris et al., 2018).

CVSS Limitations

  • Equal Weighting Issue: CVSS equally weights confidentiality, integrity, and availability, which may not be relevant for all industries. For example, healthcare systems may prioritize availability over confidentiality (Kovacs, 2018).
  • Misleading Scores: CVSS scores can be misleading for complex critical systems, as they fail to fully account for the cascading effects of vulnerabilities (Kovacs, 2018).

Cyber Risk Quantification (CRQ) as an Alternative

Cyber Risk Quantification (CRQ) allows organizations to quantify risk in financial terms, helping them better understand the potential economic impact of vulnerabilities. For example, CRQ can provide more context-specific prioritization by assigning different values based on the industry, such as hospitals or banks (Bowen et al., 2021).

Conclusion

While CVSS has been widely adopted, its limitations in context-specific applications and its failure to address the nuances of complex vulnerabilities reduce its effectiveness in certain industries. CRQ offers a more flexible and financially oriented approach, making it a robust alternative for organizations seeking to prioritize risks based on economic impact and operational needs.
