Threat Modeling and Management

Focus on various threat modeling methods and vulnerability management: it introduced popular approaches such as PASTA (Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis), CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System), ATT&CK (Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge), and OWASP (Open Web Application Security Project). I also had the opportunity to deepen my understanding of STRIDE and Attack Trees, two methods I was already familiar with, by exploring their applications in more detail.

What I Learned

  • Difference between threats and vulnerabilities: It clarified the essential distinction between these two concepts. I learned that vulnerabilities are technical weaknesses in a system, whereas threats are events or circumstances that can exploit those vulnerabilities. This helped me better understand how both interact within a security framework.
  • Threat modeling techniques: While I was already familiar with STRIDE and Attack Trees, it allowed me to explore their application frameworks and utility in specific contexts. I also discovered tools like PASTA, which offers a more comprehensive, risk-oriented approach, as well as CVSS, a standardized system for scoring vulnerabilities. Additionally, ATT&CK and OWASP complemented this exploration by providing relevant frameworks for analyzing attacks and securing web applications.
  • Practical guides for threat modeling: Through resources like the Threat Modeling Manifesto and Adam Shostackâ€™s book, I gained practical knowledge on how to select and adapt these various threat modeling techniques depending on the specific needs of a security project.

Learning Outcomes

  • Deepening my knowledge of threat modeling techniques: While I was already familiar with STRIDE and Attack Trees, it helped me better understand how and when to apply them in various contexts. For instance, STRIDE is particularly useful in complex software systems, whereas Attack Trees are effective in modeling attack scenarios in complex infrastructures.
  • Discovering new techniques: PASTA, CVSS, ATT&CK, and OWASP were key discoveries. I learned how these methods and frameworks can be used to analyze risks in a more structured and comprehensive manner. For example, PASTA allows for risk-based evaluation in complex environments, while CVSS provides a standardized framework for assessing the severity of vulnerabilities.
  • Combining techniques in a hybrid model: I also learned that combining multiple methods often leads to a more comprehensive analysis. For instance, PASTA can be combined with STRIDE or ATT&CK to provide a more complete evaluation of threats in dynamic environments.

Critical and Personal Reflections

It allowed me to deepen my understanding of techniques I was already familiar with, such as STRIDE and Attack Trees, while introducing me to new tools like PASTA, CVSS, ATT&CK, and OWASP. What I found particularly interesting was how these different approaches complement each other and can be adapted depending on the context.

However, I also realized that mastering these tools requires a deep understanding of the system in which they are applied. The challenge lies in selecting the most appropriate method for each situation. For example, while STRIDE is powerful, it can be time-consuming to implement in very complex environments. I also learned that using these tools in the early stages of development is crucial to avoid costly corrections later in the process.
