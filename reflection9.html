Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

I explored the essential concepts of Business Continuity (BC) and Disaster Recovery (DR), critical tools for risk management and organizational resilience. It focused on the creation and management of BC/DR plans, key factors such as BIA, RTO, and RPO, as well as emerging trends in information risk management.

What I Learned

  • Creating Business Continuity (BC) and Disaster Recovery (DR) Plans: ISO 22301 was highlighted as a key standard in creating BC plans, ensuring that a business can maintain critical functions in the event of major incidents.
  • Key Factors in BC/DR Plans: Concepts like RTO and RPO help assess recovery needs. For instance, RTO measures the time needed to restore critical functions, while RPO defines the maximum allowable data loss.
  • Emerging Trends in Information Risk Management: Cloud solutions and DRaaS have become vital, providing cost-effective recovery solutions that ensure fast recovery in case of major disasters.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding BC, DR, RTO, and RPO: I deepened my understanding of how these concepts interact, like how RTO/RPO requirements determine the recovery strategy and solutions like DRaaS.
  • Proposing suitable recovery solutions: Based on specific business needs, I can now suggest solutions like DRaaS for SMEs looking for reliable yet cost-efficient recovery strategies.
  • Analyzing future trends in risk management: The growing relevance of digital transformation and cloud solutions has pushed businesses to adapt their BC/DR plans continuously.

Critical and Personal Reflections

ISO 22301 provided a structured framework for developing BC/DR plans, and the relevance of disaster recovery in today's digital context became evident. However, solutions like DRaaS showed me that while effective, they can be complex to implement and require careful cost-benefit analysis.
